What You Need To Know

In this class, you will be creating the first of two assignments necessary for a buyer user to gain access to a product. This assignment is called a 'Category Product Assignment', and creates the relationship between a product and a category. Remember, a product can never be assigned directly to a user. There must be a category in-between them to create the relationship. The next class will complete the other side of this indirect relationship between the user and product by assigning the user (or group in our case) to the category.

Creating a Category Product Assignment

We will be focusing on the create.js script, but feel free to take a look at the others to see what kind of CRUD capabilities you have access to for Category Product Assignments.

Once you are in the create.js script, take a look at the object you will be creating stored in the assignment variable. As you can see, it is a pretty simple object. It has a CategoryID, ProductID, and ListOrder. Additionally, a Category Product Assignment requires a buyerID as a parameter, represented in the first line. Fill in the buyerID for the buyer you recently created. Then fill in the CategoryID and ProductID with the first of three products you have created. Run when you are ready and do it again with the two other products that you created.
